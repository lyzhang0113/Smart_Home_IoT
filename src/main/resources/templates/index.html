SmartPot

Many people worldwide have difficulties taking care of plants.
We want to change that.

How does it work?

Smart Pot is a device that will take care of your plants automatically.
Automatic
Watering

The pot will automatically water the plant with pre-set intervals.

Automatic
Lighting

The pot will automatically give light to the plant with pre-set intervals.

Remind to
Fertilize plants

The pot will remind you when to fertilize the plant.

Modular Design

The plant and the reservoir could be separated so that the user could clean the reservoir easily.

Plant Database

SmartPot comes with a built-in database with all kinds of plant data for the user to easily select the perfect default setting for any plant.

Introduce the SmartPot Team

The purpose of this subsystem is to give the device a way of watering, lighting, and possibly feeding the plant. The tasks in this system include a 3D model and print of the sprinkler head, with a consideration of the materials selected for the part itself, as well as unifying the lighting parts and the sprinkler to create a cohesive part.

Rachael Mahserjian
Head Design and Production

The pot will consist of a shell and a water resistant internal liner which will contain the plant. This subsystem will focus on ensuring that the pump system is adequate for the needs of the sprinkler design, and that the reservoir is designed in conjunction with the Piping & Water Resistance subsystem.

Brian Milano
Pump and Reservoir

This subsystem will manage the piping and water containment system from how it enters the reservoir, is propelled by the pump assembly, and exits the pot via tubing to the sprinkler head. Communication will be key for this portion of the design so that the aforementioned subsystems can all be correctly integrated into the water system as a whole.

Nicolas Wang
Piping and Water Resistance

Having learned through experience with the Mini Project and beyond, the team had developed a comprehensive understanding of the various benefits and drawbacks of different battery types. It has been discerned with full confidence that increasing voltage (by either using a larger battery or connecting small batteries in series) does not equate to a more powerful or power-efficient device.

Paul Demers
Power, Sensors and Wiring

In any system with multiple mechanical and electrical parts, it is necessary to ensure that all can be mounted to their base so there is enough room for proper operation. In this case, the Arduino, screen, battery casing, pump, and reservoir must all be contained within or around the edge of the pot so that the screen is visible, batteries are accessible, etc. Additionally, the sprinkler/lighting head must be manufactured so that it can attach to its adjustable mount, which must attach to the main pot base.

Sam Wiegand

The basis of the project’s value proposition lies in the ability to store a database of plants and their respective requirements and have the product communicate with the database effectively. The specifications of the database are detailed above, but in order for the data to be translated into something tangible, it needs to be fetched by the Arduino and translated into code.
